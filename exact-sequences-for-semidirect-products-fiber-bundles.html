§ Relationship to gauges

Let XXX be the space of all states. Let OOO be a group action whose orbits identify equivalent states. So the space of "physical states" or "states that describe the same physical scenario" is the orbit of XXX under OOO, or X/OX/OX/O. Now, the physical space X/OX/OX/O is acted upon by some group GGG. If we want to "undo the quotienting" to have GGG act on all of XXX, then we need to construct G⋉OG \ltimes OG⋉O. GGG is normal here because OOO already knows how to act on the whole space; GGG does not, so OOO needs to "guide" the action of GGG by acting on it. The data needed to construct G⋉OG \ltimes OG⋉O is a connection. Topologically, we have X→X/OX \rightarrow X/OX→X/O and G↷X/OG \curvearrowright X/OG↷X/O. We want to extend this to (G⋉O)↷X(G \ltimes O) \curvearrowright X(G⋉O)↷X. We imagine this as: 
*1| #1 | @1  X
*2| #2 | @2
*3| #3 | @3
  | |  |
  | v  |
* | #  | @ X/H
where the action of HHH permutes amongst the fibers of *, #, @. Next, we have an action of GGG on X/HX/HX/H: 
*1| #1 | @1  X
*2| #2 | @2
*3| #3 | @3
  | |  |
  | v  |
* | #  | @ [X/H] --G--> # | @ | *
We need to lift this action of H the H-orbits. This is precisely the data a connection gives us (why?) I guess the intuition is that the orbits of XXX are like the tangent spaces where X→X/OX \rightarrow X/OX→X/O is the projection from the bundle into the base space, and the GGG is a curve that tells us what the "next point" we want to travel to from the current point. The connection allows us to "lift" this to "next tangent vector". That's quite beautiful. We want the final picture to be:
